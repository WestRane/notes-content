## Captivating Drama in a Carousel of Bullying
*October 2025*

As expected, this game really tries to make you cry. Sometimes it succeeds, sometimes... not so much.

### Gameplay

It's my first time playing an "interactive movie" game, so it was unusual to see gameplay where sometimes you only need to move the mouse in the direction you've been told to move. There were many moments with genuinely interesting gameplay. When Aiden needs to move through walls, peek into documents, remember enemies' positions and such, it's pretty interesting. Also it's good to see a variety of ways to move. In some chapters you ride a bike. Sometimes you ride a horse. Good. At the same time going through corridors full of entities and flames made me yawn. Unfortunately, almost every other chapter makes you do that. The developers tried to give the impression that the game is full of variables. But it's not always like that. When you need to eliminate enemies, you might think... ok, I can possess one of them and kill the others? Right, but in most cases you have only ONE person to possess. You can't choose another one, even if they have the same gun and ability to eliminate. You can only progress the way the developers intended. It really breaks immersiveness.
  
### Characters and drama

I can't say much about characters, they are... captivating? Dawkins has his personal drama that really made me sad. This drama made him obsessed with some morally ambiguous ideas, but they are debatable as well! Ryan wasn't that great at first, but the developers let us forgive him after some time (and I forgave him after giving the story some thought), so why not. Navajo people and many other minor characters were interesting. But there were TOO many moments where scenario was like "let's make jodie bullied and let her cry for a while". Her entire childhood she was surrounded either by kind Dawkins and Cole, or by shitty teenagers (birthday party), criminal adults (bar) etc. It's too unrealistic, it wasn't even about Aiden abilities. There was no real reason for her to be bullied literally everywhere. So it feels like the developers tried too much on making you cry here.

### Conclusion

But in general it's a great story with interesting characters, somewhat ok-ish gameplay, sometimes with multiple ways to make it through the story. Worth playing for sure.
