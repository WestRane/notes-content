## The Illusion of Choice in a Scripted City
*September 2025*

My first encounter with a GTA game was when I was a kid. For me it was a game about running, shooting, carjacking and stuff. It was Vice City. I first played GTA IV back in 2016. I tried my best, but it was too boring after 10 or so hours. So I tried again after a few years. Yet the same result. So now I finally managed to complete the story in 2025, so I have some thoughts.

### Missions

Every GTA game is based on completing main story and side missions. I can count the exact number of missions that were a pure pleasure to complete. Three. Robbing a bank, the first interaction with a helicopter and kidnapping the daughter of a mobster. That's it. Some other missions weren't great, but characters were entertaining. Especially with Packie.

But the majority of missions are absolutely the same, very repetitive. The only thing that changes is the exact location of a place itself and of NPCs inside of it. You go there - you kill people - you get money - you call your employer - you repeat the same hellish cycle. Interesting? Nuh uh. You may use different paths to complete some missions (go on top of the roof of a building nearby instead of directly approaching NPCs on the ground), but it doesn't make missions good at all.

Also many of them are overly scripted. A bike is driving away from you. You have a goal of killing a person on that bike. You know what? You can shoot 1000 bullets at him and he won't die because Rockstar made him invulnerable before some point of a mission, most of the time it's not even worth it. You can't kill the driver carrying diamonds to get them, you need to wait for a driver to drive to the park and try to run, and only then he becomes killable.

And why do we do most of the missions? For two reasons: to get info about the people Nico is interested in, and getting money. Why does he need money? I spent 50 hours in this game and I still have no idea. There are no places to spend money. You can't buy property, you can't gamble, you can only buy weapons and eat burgers. Boring af.

### Characters

I cannot name some of them even though I tried really hard to read every single line in every single story. Some of the characters are so similar. Especially in the middle of a story. You meet a person, you see that he is a criminal idiot with a 0% charisma and 100% insanity. Remember Manny? Brucie? Liz? Even Little Jacob is kinda like that. They all talk boring shit 'bout their "streets", "drugs", "muscles", whatever, but nothing funny, nothing interesting. They are all too extravagant. Written to be so unlike everyone else that they became a boring disaster on screen. Playboy X wants to build kindergartens, that's why he... um... sells drugs. They are insane.

Of course it's a bit harder to judge when talking about Nico, Roman etc. Nico has a really interesting past and personality. Roman is a good friend of ours, every early mission where we help him out of trouble is a quality drama. It's also really breathtaking to hear "you remind me of me" when Nico helps Dwayne.

Also there are plenty of characters that have two-sided morality. Francis asks us to kill a drug dealer, but he also asks us to kill a journalist with compromising evidence against him. Interesting.

### Technical state.

It's easy to just say that in 2025 a somewhat modern PC can handle this game. With crashes in some missions, with the game not doing anything when it's not in fullscreen, with an insane amount of hard-coded scripts, but still. And yet it's definitely not a good side of GTA IV. It is very poorly ported on PC and it works pretty bad. Average 2008 PC suffered from this game. Rockstar Launcher crashes even more that the game itself, it cannot automatically decide which save is the most recent and such.

### Immersiveness

Let's just say... I was dating Kiki most of the game. So Nico had a girlfriend. So what does he say to Roman? Yep, he says that he is interested in Kate McReary. Because Rockstar doesn't give a shit about my Nico having Kiki as a girlfriend. There are really many examples like that. You can't order food in restaurants. You can't go into most of the buildings. You can steal a police car and get the exact location of any criminal in their db. Characters tell you exact locations of everything, even Mallorie literally knows where Roman was taken by russians. At the same time design of different parts of Liberty City is really good and makes you feel like you drive a real city. Kiki stalks you when you're with other girls. Also decisions (choices) are kinda interesting. I spent many minutes thinking about whether I should kill a person or let him go. From a girl that cheated on Dwayne and to Darko. Most of these decisions don't change the story at all, but they are really entertaining ngl.

### Conclusion

So... we have a poorly coded game. With an average immersiveness (great details mixed with big flaws), a beautifully designed Liberty City. With some characters being insanely boring and some characters being pretty ok-ish. A game about killing people to get info about people to kill them as well. Interesting plot and story, but a pretty average result. At first 15 hours I was entertained, at second 15 hours I was really bored and hoped the game will end asap. At the last 15 hours I was enjoying a story with Pegorino, McRearys and such, but still hoped that it won't last long. So an aftertaste isn't great, but isn't bad. I was really interested in how Nico will build his life in a new town, but... he just kills, then kills, and then kills. And only in the end of the game we finally see a big progress in a character development. I really want to recommend this game 'cause of its interesting story, but these 50 hours weren't worth it. I kinda expected something better based on the first 5 hours of the game.
